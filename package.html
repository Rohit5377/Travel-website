Top Destination

Taj Mahal, Agra

One of the most iconic monuments in the world, the Taj Mahal is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a symbol of eternal love. Built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal, this white marble mausoleum is renowned for its stunning architecture and intricate craftsmanship.

Book Now

Ellora and Ajanta Caves, Maharashtra

These UNESCO World Heritage Sites are renowned for their ancient rock-cut temples and caves adorned with intricate sculptures and paintings. Visitors can marvel at the architectural brilliance and spiritual significance of these historic sites.

Book Now

Rishikesh, Uttarakhand

Nestled in the foothills of the Himalayas, Rishikesh is a spiritual hub and adventure destination. Visitors can practice yoga and meditation, attend Ganga Aarti on the banks of the sacred Ganges River, and enjoy thrilling activities like white-water rafting and bungee jumping.

Book Now

Mahabalipuram, Tamil Nadu

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Mahabalipuram is renowned for its ancient rock-cut temples, cave sanctuaries, and intricately carved sculptures. Visitors can explore landmarks like the Shore Temple, Arjuna's Penance, and the Five Rathas, all showcasing exquisite Dravidian architecture.

Book Now

Manali, Himachal Pradesh

Situated in the heart of the Himalayas, Manali is a popular hill station known for its scenic beauty and adventurous activities. The Beas River flowing through Manali offers thrilling opportunities for river rafting, with rapids ranging from gentle to challenging, making it suitable for experienced rafters.

Book Now

Ranthambore National Park, Rajasthan

One of the best places in India to spot the majestic Bengal tiger, Ranthambore National Park is a haven for wildlife enthusiasts and photographers. Travelers can embark on thrilling safaris to observe tigers, leopards, sloth bears, and a variety of bird species.

Book Now

Jaipur, Rajasthan

Known as the "Pink City," Jaipur is famous for its historic forts, palaces, and vibrant bazaars. Visitors can explore landmarks like the Amber Fort, City Palace, and Hawa Mahal, and immerse themselves in the rich culture and heritage of Rajasthan

Book Now

Leh-Ladakh, Jammu and Kashmir

A land of rugged landscapes, and ancient monasteries, Leh-Ladakh is a paradise for adventure seekers and nature lovers. Travelers can trek in the Himalayas, ride through dramatic mountain passes.

Book Now

Jaisalmer, Rajasthan

Known as the "Golden City," Jaisalmer is a captivating desert destination located in the heart of the Thar Desert in Rajasthan. This ancient city is famous for its majestic sandstone fort, exquisite havelis (mansions), and vast stretches of golden sand dunes.

Book Now

Goa

India's smallest state is famous for its stunning beaches, vibrant nightlife, and Portuguese heritage. Visitors can relax on sandy shores, explore historic churches and forts, indulge in water sports, and savor delicious seafood and Goan cuisine.

Book Now

Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh

Considered one of the oldest inhabited cities in the world, Varanasi is a sacred pilgrimage site for Hindus. Situated on the banks of the Ganges River, it is known for its ghats , as well as its bustling narrow streets filled with temples and markets.

Book Now

LMunnar, Kerala

Nestled in the Western Ghats of Kerala, Munnar is a picturesque hill station renowned for its lush greenery, tea plantations, and diverse flora and fauna. The forests surrounding Munnar offer excellent opportunities for adventure activities like trekking.
