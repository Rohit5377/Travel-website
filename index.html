Taj Mahal, Agra

One of the most iconic monuments in the world, the Taj Mahal is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a symbol of eternal love. Built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal, this white marble mausoleum is renowned for its stunning architecture and intricate craftsmanship.

Ellora and Ajanta Caves, Maharashtra

These UNESCO World Heritage Sites are renowned for their ancient rock-cut temples and caves adorned with intricate sculptures and paintings. Visitors can marvel at the architectural brilliance and spiritual significance of these historic sites.

Book Now

Rishikesh, Uttarakhand

Nestled in the foothills of the Himalayas, Rishikesh is a spiritual hub and adventure destination. Visitors can practice yoga and meditation, attend Ganga Aarti on the banks of the sacred Ganges River, and enjoy thrilling activities like white-water rafting.
